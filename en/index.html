Gridlab is the power systems laboratory and power systems research group of the Institute of Sustainable Energy and School of Engineering at HES-SO Valais-Wallis, in Sion, Switzerland. We research and develop modeling and decision-support tools for secure and cost-effective integration of renewables into power grids and electrification of demand. We also prepare the future generation of electrical engineers at the bachelor's, master's and PhD levels with exciting training programs and cutting-edge research projects.

Experimental Infrastructure

With over 250 kVA of installed experimental infrastructure and 120 km among real and emulated power lines, Gridlab is equipped to perform a wide range of experiments, including power hardware-in-the-loop simulations, energy storage testing, and renewable energy integration studies.

arrow_right_alt
Dispatch

Our reduced-scale platform to test active and reactive power control in power grids, control of rotating machines, frequency dynamics, islanded grids and inertia-less systems.

arrow_right_alt
District

Four low-voltage distribution grids reproduced in a 1:1 scale to test control of distributed energy resources in presence of increased electrification levels and photovoltaic generation.

Fabrizio Sossan

Associate Professor and Head of Gridlab

Fabrizio Sossanclose

Fabrizio holds a Master's in Computer Engineering (University of Genova, 2010) and a PhD in Electrical Engineering (Technical University of Denmark DTU, 2014). Before joining HES-SO, Fabrizio held a professorship appointment at MINES Paris and researcher positions at EPFL, ETHZ, and NREL (USA) as visiting researchers, working on grid integration of renewable energies and electrification. In 2020, he cofounded ModBESS, a US-based company developing energy management software for energy resources. Fabrizio is the coordinator and principal investigator of the Innosuisse Flagship project STORE, and a work-package leader in the Horizon Europe project STOR-HY on control of pumped-storage hydropower.

Guo Baoling

Senior Researcher

Guo Baolingclose

Baoling received bachelor’s and master’s degrees in electrical engineering from China University of Petroleum in 2012 and 2014, respectively. She completed an International Master’s in Smart Grids and Buildings at Grenoble Institute of Technology (Grenoble-INP), France, in 2015, and her Ph.D. in Electrical Engineering from the University Grenoble Alpes, France, in 2019. Following her doctoral studies, she undertook a one-year postdoctoral fellowship at G2Elab in Grenoble and a two-year postdoctoral position at GridLab, HES-SO Valais. She is now a senior researcher at HES-SO Valais. Baoling has been involved in numerous research and industrial applied projects and actively contributes to Federal and Horizon Europe proposal submissions. Her main research interests include the modeling and control of power electronic systems, the regulation of electric vehicle chargers and photovoltaic installations in LV/MV distribution networks, and the smart grid solutions for railway systems.

Partners and funding

We collaborate with a wide range of national and international partners across the whole value chain of the power industry, from consumers and producers to grid operators. Our research is supported by the Swiss Innovation Agency Innosuisse, the Swiss Federal Office of Energy, Horizon Europe, and industrial partners. We are member of DERlab.
